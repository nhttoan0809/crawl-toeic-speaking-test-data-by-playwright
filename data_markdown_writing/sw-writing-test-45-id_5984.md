## 2. Respond to a written request

**Request 1**

```text
Direction: Read the e-mail below.
From: Mark Johnson
To: Facilities Maintenance Team
Subject: Air Conditioning Issue
Dear Facilities Team,
I’m writing to let you know that the air conditioning in our office isn’t working properly. It’s been very warm over the past few days, and it’s becoming hard for us to concentrate on work. Could you please check on this issue and let us know what can be done to fix it?
Thank you for your prompt attention.
Best regards,
Mark Johnson
Directions: Respond to the e-mail as if you are a member of the Facilities Maintenance Team. In your e-mail, provide TWO pieces of information and make ONE suggestion.
```

**Request 2**

```text
Direction: Read the e-mail below.
From: Kim Palmer, The Daily Times
To: Paul Woods
Subject: Interview Availability
Mr. Woods,
We at the Daily Times would like to interview you for an article about your recently published research. We aim to publish the article in next Sunday’s newspaper, so if you have time today or tomorrow, we can come to your office at your research facility to meet you.
Kim Palmer
Writer
The Daily Times
Directions: Respond to the e-mail as if you are Paul Woods. In your e-mail, ask TWO questions and provide ONE piece of information.
```
